Post Basic B.Sc Nursing

What Exactly is Post Basic B.Sc Nursing?

Post Basic B.Sc Nursing is a two-year undergraduate degree program designed for Registered Nurses (RNs) who have already completed a General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) course. This program provides advanced knowledge and skills in nursing, allowing nurses to upgrade their qualifications, enhance their clinical competencies, and prepare for leadership roles in the healthcare industry. It also serves as a bridge course for those looking to pursue higher education in nursing, such as a Masterâ€™s in Nursing (M.Sc Nursing).

What Are the Required Eligibility Conditions to Join Post Basic B.Sc Nursing Course?

To be eligible for the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing course, candidates typically need to meet the following criteria:

  • Educational Qualification: Must have completed the General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) course from an institution recognized by the Indian Nursing Council (INC).
  • Registration: Must be a Registered Nurse (RN) and Registered Midwife (RM) with the State Nursing Council.
  • Experience: Some institutions may require candidates to have a certain amount of work experience as a nurse, though this is not always mandatory.
  • Minimum Age: Candidates should generally be at least 21 years old, though there is usually no upper age limit.

What Are the Skills Required to Do Post Basic B.Sc Nursing Course?

Nurses pursuing a Post Basic B.Sc Nursing course should possess the following skills:

  • Advanced Clinical Skills: In-depth knowledge of nursing procedures and patient care.
  • Leadership Abilities: Skills in managing healthcare teams and making informed decisions.
  • Communication Skills: Strong verbal and written communication for interacting with patients, families, and healthcare professionals.
  • Critical Thinking: The ability to assess situations, solve problems, and make decisions quickly.
  • Empathy and Compassion: A caring attitude towards patients and a commitment to improving their health.
  • Technical Proficiency: Familiarity with medical technology and equipment.
  • Time Management: Efficiently managing time and tasks in a busy healthcare environment.

How to Get Admission to the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing Course?

The admission process for the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing course typically involves the following steps:

  • Research Institutions: Identify nursing colleges recognized by the Indian Nursing Council (INC) that offer the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing course.
  • Application Process: Submit an application to the chosen institution, either online or offline, providing necessary documents like GNM certificates, RN/RM registration, and work experience proof if required.
  • Entrance Exams: Some institutions may conduct entrance exams to assess the candidates' knowledge and aptitude for the course. Prepare for these exams if applicable.
  • Selection Process: Selection may be based on merit (marks obtained in the GNM course and/or entrance exam) or through a counseling process.
  • Final Admission: Once selected, complete the admission process by paying the required fees and submitting all necessary documentation.

What is the Syllabus of the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing Course?

The syllabus for the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing course generally covers the following subjects over two years:

  • 1st Year:
    • Nursing Foundation
    • Nutrition and Dietetics
    • Biochemistry and Biophysics
    • Psychology
    • Microbiology
    • Maternal Nursing
    • Child Health Nursing
    • Medical-Surgical Nursing (Adult including Geriatrics)
  • 2nd Year:
    • Mental Health Nursing
    • Community Health Nursing
    • Nursing Education
    • Introduction to Nursing Administration
    • Introduction to Nursing Research and Statistics

The course also includes practical training and internships in various healthcare settings, allowing students to apply their theoretical knowledge in real-world scenarios.

What is the Fee Structure of the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing Course?

The fee structure for the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing course varies depending on the institution:

  • Government Colleges: Fees typically range from INR 20,000 to INR 1 lakh per year.
  • Private Colleges: Fees can range from INR 1 lakh to INR 3 lakh per year.

Additional costs may include hostel fees, lab fees, and examination fees.

What Are the Career Prospects and Job Opportunities After Completion of the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing Course?

Graduates of the Post Basic B.Sc Nursing course have enhanced career opportunities in various sectors, including:

  • Hospitals and Clinics: Roles as Senior Nurses, Nurse Managers, or Nursing Supervisors.
  • Community Health: Positions in community health programs, NGOs, or public health departments.
  • Education: Opportunities as Nursing Educators, Lecturers, or Clinical Instructors in nursing schools and colleges.
  • Specialized Nursing Fields: Advanced roles in areas like Intensive Care, Emergency Nursing, Pediatric Nursing, or Psychiatric Nursing.
  • Healthcare Administration: Positions in nursing administration or hospital management.
  • Research: Roles in nursing research or clinical trials.
  • Government Jobs: Positions in government hospitals, military nursing services, or public health initiatives.

What Are the Expected Pay Packages or Salaries After Doing a Post Basic B.Sc Nursing Degree?

The salary for Post Basic B.Sc Nursing graduates can vary based on experience, location, and the type of healthcare facility:

  • Entry-Level (Fresh Graduates): INR 3 lakh to INR 5 lakh per annum.
  • With 2-5 Years of Experience: INR 4 lakh to INR 7 lakh per annum.
  • Senior Positions (Nurse Manager, Nursing Educator): INR 6 lakh to INR 10 lakh per annum or more.
  • International Opportunities: Nurses working abroad, particularly in countries like the USA, Canada, or the Middle East, can earn significantly higher salaries, ranging from INR 25 lakh to INR 60 lakh per annum or more, depending on the country and healthcare system.
